Bowsaw Lake

Bowsaw Lake is our larger 1.7 acre lake and is home to carp, tench and chub. The biggest mirror carp we’ve had caught on this lake was 36lb with numerous double figure mirror and common carp showing most of the year. The largest tench caught on this lake was 7lb 8oz. We’ve just completed a restocking programme of mirror and common carp weighing between 15lb and 25lb in this lake which should boost the chances of a big catch even more! The match weight record for this lake is 301lb.

We’d love to hear your reports and see pictures of your catches. Please contact us with your news and views to add to these pages!

Competition and match bookings for opens and club days are available please click on either the upcoming matches or book this lake buttons below to see what’s coming up or to book this lake for your own match.

Billhook Lake

Billhook Lake is our smaller 1.4 acre lake and is home to carp, tench, bream, roach, perch, barbell, chub, ide and F1’s averaging 1- 4lb. In addition to Bowsaw Lake there are also some very large carp in this lake with weights between 4lb-23lb. This lake makes for a fantastic match lake but also is also brilliant for a day of pleasure fishing. The match weight record for this lake is 284lb for a 5 hour match.

We’d love to hear your reports and see pictures of your catches on Billhook Lake. Please contact us with your news and views to add to these pages!

Location

From the M3

Take Junction 5 (Hook turn off) and follow the signs to Odiham. Get into Odiham High Street and take a right turn by the Kings Restaurant into Kings Road carry on until you come to the Odiham Cricket Club on your Left and just passed that take the next turn Left sign posted Roke and Park Corner. Take the next right carrying on for about 2 miles you will come to Roke Farm either side of the road and carry on passed that for about 500 yards and Bowsaw and Billhook lake is on your right hand side and is sign posted.

From the A3

Get on to the Hogs Back A31 and then follow signs for Farnham when you get to the Shepherd and flock roundabout follow signs for Odiham. Once in Odiham High Street, take a left turn by the Kings Restaurant into Kings Road carry on until you come to the Odiham Cricket Club on your Left and just passed that take the next turn Left sign posted Roke and Park Corner. Take the next right carrying on for about 2 miles you will come to Roke Farm either side of the road and carry on passed that for about 500 yards and Bowsaw and Billhook lake is on your right hand side and is sign posted.
